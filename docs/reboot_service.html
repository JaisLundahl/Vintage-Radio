Reboot Service Technical Deep Dive

This service manages the critical final stages of the device lifecycle, ensuring that file systems are unmounted, hardware is silenced, and the battery is protected during power-down or restart events.

1. Graceful Mode Termination

stopMode(AppMode m)

A private helper function that acts as a central dispatcher for shutting down active application logic.

  • Mode Specificity: It identifies which service is currently active (BT, MP3, or NET) and calls its specific stop routine.
  • Data Integrity: By calling these functions, the service ensures that MP3 history is written to the SD card and network streams are closed cleanly before the power is cut.

2. Controlled System Restart

rebootFromMode(AppMode currentMode)

Initiates a standard software reset while ensuring the hardware remains in a known good state.

  1. Audio Lockdown: Calls audioForceShutdown() to immediately stop I2S clocks and silence the amplifier.
  2. Radio Cleanup: Disconnects and powers off the WiFi radio to prevent transceiver hang-ups during reboot.
  3. Storage Safety: Unmounts the SD card (SD.end()) to prevent filesystem corruption.
  4. Hardware Reset: Calls esp_restart() to perform a full chip reset.

3. Low-Voltage Protection

safeShutdown(AppMode currentMode)

A specialized emergency routine triggered when the system detects a critical battery level (under 12V).

Priority: Mute the amplifier immediately to reduce instantaneous current draw, effectively extending the remaining capacitor energy for final SD card writes.
  • Power Conservation: Like the reboot routine, it tears down all active services and unmounts the SD card.
  • Infinite Halt: Instead of restarting, it enters an infinite while(true) loop after logging "Goodnight!" to the console. This prevents the system from oscillating between booting and low-voltage shutdowns, which can damage batteries.

4. Coordination with Other Services

The RebootService acts as the "orchestrator" for all other modules during a shutdown event:

  • AudioService: Relies on audioForceShutdown and audioMute.
  • InputService: Uses the AppMode enum to identify the current system state.
  • External Modes: Directly interfaces with mode_bt, mode_mp3, mode_net, and mode_idle.
